Your Morning Briefing

MSN UK 1 hour ago News desk

Good morning. Here’s what you need to know to start your day with our daily briefing, compiled by Liji Varghese.

Zelensky urges Ukraine to 'hold on' as Russia blockades Black Sea coast

© 2022 Laura Lezza President Volodymyr Zelensky has urged Ukraine to 'hold on' as Russia was said to have blockaded Ukraine’s Black Sea coast. Meanwhile, reports emerged on Monday that Russia has asked China for military equipment to use in its invasion of Ukraine. (Evening Standard)

Labour demands action as families face almost £400 annual rise in petrol

© 2022 Huw Fairclough Labour is calling on the government to act over the “spiralling cost-of-living crisis” as figures suggest families face a near £400 annual rise in the cost of petrol. The party said its analysis of official statistics found the average family is facing a yearly rise of £386 on the cost of petrol. (The Independent)

The Power of the Dog wins best picture and director at Baftas 

The Power of the Dog, Jane Campion’s repressed western starring Benedict Cumberbatch as a ferocious rancher in 1925 Montana, has taken the top two prizes at this year’s Baftas, winning best film and best director. Best actress was won by Joanna Scanlan, the veteran Welsh star of stage and small screen for her astonishing performance as a bereaved Muslim convert in Aleem Khan’s debut, After Love. (The Guardian)

Royal Family to attend Commonwealth Day service without the Queen

© PA Archive Commonwealth Day 2021 The Royal Family will attend the annual Commonwealth Day service but the Queen will miss the event for the first time in almost a decade. The Queen, who has recently recovered from a bout of Covid, had hoped to attend the important event in the royal calendar alongside the Prince of Wales, Duchess of Cornwall and Duke and Duchess of Cambridge. (Evening Standard)

In depth: Climate justice could be the answer to the cost of living crisis

© 2022 Getty Images The rising cost of simply existing will be crippling for many, squeezing the last pennies out of our already frayed pockets. The climate crisis is a “threat multiplier”, meaning that it makes pre-existing problems far, far worse. There is now research that suggests the cost of climate change could be six times higher than previously thought. (The Face)

Video of the Day: White rhino named Queenie to mark Queen's Platinum Jubilee

© Press Association A newborn white rhino at Cotswold Wildlife Park and Gardens in Burford, Oxfordshire, has been named Queenie to mark the monarch's Platinum Jubilee year. (Press Association)
